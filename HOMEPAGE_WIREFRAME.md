# A2F Homepage Wireframe

## Purpose

The homepage should quickly answer three questions:

1. What is A2F?
2. Which location / experience is right for me?
3. What should I do next?

This page is not meant to explain everything equally. Its job is to direct visitors into either the `Private Training` path or the `Public Gym` path with confidence.

## Primary Homepage Goals

- introduce the A2F brand as one company with two offerings
- make the private/public difference obvious within the first screen or two
- create clean pathways into the right conversion funnel
- build trust with results, environment, and credibility
- support mobile users without overwhelming them

## Visual Direction

- black / charcoal foundation
- strong red action color
- white typography
- bold uppercase section headers using `Oswald`
- cleaner spacing and sharper content hierarchy than the current site
- photo-driven layout with disciplined use of overlays and panels

## Wireframe Structure

## 1. Header

### Contents

- A2F logo
- primary nav
- high-visibility CTA button

### Nav

- Home
- Locations
- Private Training
- Public Gym
- Memberships
- Businesses at A2F
- About
- Contact

### Primary Header CTA

- `Book Consultation`

### Secondary Mobile CTA Option

- `Join Now`

### UX Notes

- desktop nav should stay simple and direct
- mobile nav should keep the two main funnels prominent near the top
- CTA button should not compete with too many alternatives

## 2. Hero Section

### Goal

Immediately introduce A2F as a results-focused fitness brand with two clear paths.

### Layout

- full-width hero image or layered collage
- dark overlay for readability
- centered or left-aligned content block
- two strong CTA buttons

### Recommended Content Structure

Eyebrow:

- `Two Locations. One Results-Driven Brand.`

Headline:

- `Training, Memberships, and Real Results Built the A2F Way`

Supporting copy:

- `Whether you want elite coaching or a high-performance gym environment, A2F gives you a clear path to train with purpose.`

Primary CTAs:

- `Explore Private Training`
- `Explore Public Gym`

### UX Notes

- this section must make the two-path structure obvious right away
- avoid generic gym language that could describe any facility
- use photography that shows both coaching and gym-floor energy if possible

## 3. Two-Path Split Section

### Goal

Help visitors self-identify quickly without reading long paragraphs.

### Layout

- two large stacked cards on mobile
- two side-by-side cards on desktop

### Left Card: Private Training

Suggested label:

- `Private Training`

Suggested heading:

- `Coaching, Accountability, and Customized Results`

Suggested bullets:

- personal training
- semi-private training
- competition prep
- online coaching
- meal planning

CTA:

- `Start Private Training`

### Right Card: Public Gym

Suggested label:

- `Public Gym`

Suggested heading:

- `Membership Access in a Results-Driven Environment`

Suggested bullets:

- key fob access
- WOW workouts
- InBody tracking
- top-tier equipment
- 1-on-1 training available

CTA:

- `View Memberships`

### UX Notes

- these cards should not feel identical
- private card can feel more premium and guided
- public card can feel more energetic and membership-led

## 4. Why A2F Section

### Goal

Explain the deeper reason people should trust this brand over a generic competitor.

### Layout

- short intro copy
- 4 to 6 value pillars

### Suggested Pillars

- Real Accountability
- Structured Training
- Community That Pushes You
- Measurable Progress
- Experienced Coaching
- Environment Built for Results

### Suggested Supporting Copy

- `A2F is built for people who want more than access. We create structure, support, and an environment that makes consistency easier.`

### UX Notes

- keep this section crisp
- use icons or stat-style blocks, not long paragraphs

## 5. Locations Snapshot

### Goal

Show that the brand now has two distinct locations and help users orient themselves fast.

### Layout

- two location panels
- address, hours, quick summary

### Card Content Structure

For each location:

- location name
- short summary
- address
- hours
- who it is for
- CTA

### Example Positioning

Private location:

- `Best for guided coaching, body transformation, prep, and accountability`

Public location:

- `Best for memberships, independent training, day passes, and structured weekly programming`

## 6. Featured Public Gym Benefits

### Goal

Introduce the most compelling parts of the new gym so the second location feels like a major upgrade to the brand story.

### Layout

- visual plus bullet grid

### Feature List

- Bi-Weekly InBody Composition Analysis
- WOW: Workouts of the Week
- Exclusive Key Fob Access
- Top-Tier Equipment
- Personal 1-on-1 Training Available

### Supporting Copy Direction

- `This is not just open access. It is a focused environment designed to help members train with more clarity and consistency.`

### CTA

- `See Membership Options`

## 7. Results / Testimonials Section

### Goal

Build credibility with proof from both sides of the business.

### Layout

- testimonial slider or 2-column grid
- optionally split by `Private` and `Public`

### Content Recommendation

- 2 to 3 testimonials from private coaching clients
- 1 to 2 testimonials from public gym members once available

### UX Notes

- keep testimonials short and outcome-focused
- pair with faces or photos if approved

## 8. Businesses at A2F Preview

### Goal

Introduce the internal units as a differentiator for the public gym.

### Layout

- short intro
- 3 to 5 placeholder cards or teaser slots

### Suggested Messaging

- `The new A2F Public Gym is more than a training floor. It is being built as an ecosystem of businesses that support performance, recovery, and member experience.`

### CTA

- `Explore Businesses at A2F`

### UX Notes

- if the businesses are not finalized yet, this can launch as a teaser and expand later

## 9. About / Brand Story Teaser

### Goal

Humanize the brand and connect the two-location model back to the founders.

### Layout

- photo + short story block

### Suggested Messaging Direction

- how A2F started
- why results and accountability matter
- why the second location exists

### CTA

- `Learn About A2F`

## 10. Final Conversion Section

### Goal

Give visitors a clear next step after they have seen the full homepage.

### Layout

- bold dark section
- 2 or 3 CTA blocks

### CTA Options

- `Book a Private Consultation`
- `Join the Public Gym`
- `Schedule a Tour`

### UX Notes

- this should feel decisive
- no vague “learn more” language here

## 11. Footer

### Contents

- logo
- quick nav
- both locations
- phone / email
- waiver link
- social links
- copyright

## Mobile Priorities

### Must Be Easy on Mobile

- understanding the two-path split
- tapping into the correct funnel
- calling or contacting
- viewing pricing
- getting directions

### Mobile Content Rules

- keep hero copy short
- keep split cards highly tappable
- reduce paragraph density
- preserve CTA visibility throughout the page

## Homepage Content Checklist

### Needed Before Final Design

- final homepage headline preference
- both location addresses
- updated hours for both locations
- best photos for private gym
- photos or renderings for public gym
- testimonials
- owner bios / story snippet
- final public-gym naming decision
- internal business names and descriptions

## Recommended Build Order

1. Header and homepage hero
2. Two-path split section
3. Locations section
4. Public gym benefits section
5. Testimonials
6. Final CTA and footer

## Notes for Final UI Design

- do not bury the split between private and public below the fold
- keep the homepage focused on direction, not exhaustive detail
- use the current A2F colors and type system, but elevate the layout quality significantly
- photos will matter heavily on this site, so image selection should be treated as a strategic part of the design
