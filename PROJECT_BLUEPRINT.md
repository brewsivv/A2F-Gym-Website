# A2F Website Project Blueprint

## Project Goal

Build a modernized `a2fgym.com` that supports two distinct business models under one brand:

- `A2F Private Gym`: coaching-led, transformation-focused, premium training experience
- `A2F Public Gym`: membership-led, results-driven gym access with internal partner businesses

The site should help visitors self-select quickly, understand the difference between the two locations, and take the right next action without confusion.

## Core Positioning

### Brand Architecture

Use one parent brand, `A2F`, with two clear paths:

- `Private Training`
- `Public Gym`

This should feel like one company with two experiences, not two unrelated websites.

### Audience Split

#### A2F Private Gym

Ideal for people looking for:

- personal training
- semi-private training
- competition prep
- online coaching
- meal planning
- accountability and transformation support

Primary intent:

- book a consultation
- learn about coaching options
- trust the trainers and process

#### A2F Public Gym

Ideal for people looking for:

- gym memberships
- day passes
- key fob access
- a structured workout environment
- top-tier equipment
- optional 1-on-1 training
- access to complementary in-house businesses

Primary intent:

- join now
- compare membership options
- book a tour

- Dark alt background: `#292929`

- White text: `#FFFFFF`
- Muted gray: `#999999`

Typography from current site:

- Body font: `Open Sans`
- Heading font: `Oswald`

### Visual Direction Recommendation

Keep the current bold, high-contrast gym aesthetic, but refine it:

- preserve the black / charcoal / red foundation
- use more intentional spacing and hierarchy
- keep strong uppercase headings for impact
- avoid making every section equally loud
- let `Private` feel more premium and guided
- let `Public` feel more energetic, accessible, and performance-driven
